What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser when you visit a website. They let a site remember information between page loads or visits — like whether you've dismissed a notice, or which pages you've viewed — and are a standard part of how almost all modern websites function.

Managing & disabling cookies

You can block, delete or be notified about cookies through your browser's settings — the exact steps vary by browser, but are generally found under "Privacy" or "Cookies" in settings or preferences. Blocking essential cookies may affect some site functionality, such as the mobile menu or a dismissed notice reappearing on every visit; blocking analytics cookies has no effect on how the site works for you.

  • Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions

Do Not Track signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" signal. There's currently no consistent industry standard for how sites should respond to it, so like most sites, we don't currently alter our analytics behaviour based on that signal — but since we don't run advertising cookies or cross-site tracking in the first place, the practical difference is minimal.
