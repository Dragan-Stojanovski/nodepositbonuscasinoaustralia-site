Is it legal to claim a no deposit bonus in NSW?

Online gambling law in Australia is set federally, not by individual states, so New South Wales does not have its own separate rules for offshore online casinos. The governing law is the federal Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (IGA), which prohibits companies from offering real-money online casino games to Australians without an Australian licence — a licence that, in practice, no domestic operator holds for casino-style games. It does not criminalise an individual for playing at an offshore-licensed site. This is why operators such as Bitkingz and Crocoslots, licensed respectively by the Tobique Gaming Commission and the Curaçao Gaming Control Board, can accept New South Wales residents. Note: this is general information, not legal advice. NSW's own gambling laws (the Gaming Machines Act 2001 and Casino Control Act 1992) govern licensed venues physically located in the state, such as pubs, clubs and Sydney's casinos — they don't extend to offshore online casino operators.

How gambling is regulated in NSW

New South Wales has one of the most closely watched gambling regulatory environments in the country. Land-based casinos in the state fall under the NSW Independent Casino Commission (NICC), a dedicated regulator created in 2022 in direct response to the findings of the Bergin Inquiry and later reviews into Star Entertainment's Sydney casino, which uncovered serious failures in anti-money-laundering controls and responsible gambling practices. The NICC now has significantly stronger enforcement powers than the body it replaced, including the ability to suspend a casino licence.

Outside of casinos, poker machines in pubs and clubs are regulated separately under the Gaming Machines Act 2001, overseen by Liquor & Gaming NSW. New South Wales has more poker machines than any other Australian state — over 86,000 across its pubs and clubs — which has made it a focal point for national debate on gambling harm minimisation, including trials of cashless gaming cards and mandatory pre-commitment technology.

Responsible gambling support in NSW

The NSW Government funds GambleAware NSW, a free, confidential counselling and support service with a network of specialist services across the state, alongside financial counselling for people affected by their own or someone else's gambling. This sits alongside the national Gambling Help Online service and the BetStop National Self-Exclusion Register, which lets anyone block themselves from all licensed Australian wagering services in one step.
