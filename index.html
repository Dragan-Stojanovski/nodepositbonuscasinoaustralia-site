How a no deposit bonus actually works

A no deposit bonus is exactly what it sounds like — a casino credits your account with free spins or a small amount of bonus credit before you've put any of your own money in, usually in exchange for registering and confirming your identity is real via email (and sometimes phone) verification rather than a deposit. It's a genuinely no-risk way to test a casino's registration process, game library and payout speed before you decide whether it's worth depositing into. Here's the lifecycle, from claim to cashout:

  1. Register. You create an account with your name, date of birth, email and a password, and enter the bonus code (FS100 for the offers on this site) either during sign-up or in the bonus tab afterwards.
  2. Verify. Since there's no deposit to confirm you're a real player, the casino sends a confirmation link to your email. Click it, and the spins or bonus credit land in your account — usually within a minute or two.
  3. Play. You play through the spins or bonus credit on the specific game (or game set) the offer is tied to. Any winnings go into a separate bonus balance, not your withdrawable balance.
  4. Clear the wagering requirement. Before those winnings become real, withdrawable money, you typically need to wager them (or the total bonus amount) a set number of times — the "wagering requirement" or "playthrough" — in eligible games.
  5. Cash out, up to the cap. Once cleared, winnings move to your real balance and you can request a withdrawal, usually subject to a maximum cashout limit that applies specifically to no deposit offers.

Frequently asked questions

Is a no deposit bonus really free?

Yes, in the sense that you don't stake your own money to receive it. Winnings are still subject to wagering requirements and a maximum cashout, so treat it as a free trial rather than free cash.

Why was my bonus not credited?

Most commonly: an unverified email, an incorrectly entered code, or the offer having expired or been geo-restricted. Contact support with your registration details.

Do I need to deposit eventually?

No — you can play out a no deposit bonus and withdraw any winnings within the terms without ever depositing, though many players choose to deposit once they've tried the casino.

Are FS100 offers a one-time thing per casino?

Almost always yes — no deposit bonuses are typically limited to one per person, household or payment method, enforced via KYC checks.

Is online casino play legal in Australia?

Australians can legally play at offshore-licensed online casinos; the Interactive Gambling Act restricts operators from offering real-money casino games to Australians onshore, not players from accessing licensed overseas sites.
