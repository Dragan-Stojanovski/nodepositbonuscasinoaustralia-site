How slot volatility works

Volatility describes how spread out a slot's outcomes are — not how "generous" it is overall.

Low volatility

Frequent, smaller wins. Over a fixed number of spins (like a 100-spin free spins batch), this tends to produce a steadier, more predictable result.

High volatility

Rare but larger wins. Over the same 100 spins, you're more likely to end with either nothing or a standout result — higher ceiling, higher chance of a flat outcome.

Why this matters for free spins offers

A fixed batch of free spins is a small sample size. Lower-volatility slots (like the one behind the current FS100 offer) generally give a more representative "typical" outcome across 100 spins than a high-volatility title would.
