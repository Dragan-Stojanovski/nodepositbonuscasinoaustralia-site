How to verify your email at an online casino

Email verification is the single step that most often triggers a no deposit bonus to actually credit — skip it, or miss the confirmation email, and your spins will stay invisible even though you've registered correctly. Here's exactly how to get it done.

Why email verification matters

Casinos use email confirmation as a basic anti-fraud check — it proves the account belongs to a real, reachable person before any bonus funds move. For a no deposit offer like FS100, this is usually the exact moment the system releases your free spins into the bonus balance, which is why an unverified email is the most common reason a bonus appears to be "missing" when it's actually just waiting on this one step.

Step by step

  1. Complete registration with an email address you can access immediately.
  2. Open your inbox within a few minutes — most confirmation emails arrive almost instantly.
  3. Click the verification link inside the email from the casino.
  4. Return to the casino tab and refresh, or log back in if it signed you out.
  5. Check your bonus balance — your no deposit spins or credit should now be visible.

If the email doesn't arrive

Likely causes

  • It landed in your spam or junk folder
  • You typed your email address incorrectly at sign-up
  • Your email provider is delaying delivery

Fixes

  • Check spam/junk before doing anything else
  • Use the "resend verification email" option in your account settings
  • Contact live support to confirm the email on file is correct

Frequently asked questions

Does verifying my email also verify my identity?

No — email verification just confirms your inbox is real. Identity (KYC) verification with photo ID happens separately, usually before your first withdrawal. See our KYC guide.

Can I still register if I use a disposable email address?

Most casinos block known disposable/temporary email domains, since a real, ongoing account requires a real inbox for account and security notices.

What if the verification link has expired?

Request a new one from your account settings or contact support — it will generate a fresh, working link.
