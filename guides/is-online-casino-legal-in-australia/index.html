Is online casino play legal in Australia?

Australia's gambling law targets operators, not players. No Australian has ever been prosecuted for playing at an offshore-licensed online casino — here's what the law actually says and what it means in practice.

What the Interactive Gambling Act actually says

The Interactive Gambling Act 2001 (IGA) makes it illegal for a company to offer real money online casino games to Australian residents without an Australian licence — which, in practice, no domestic operator holds for casino-style games. It does not make it an offence for an individual to play at an offshore-licensed site. This is why offshore casinos like Bitkingz (Tobique Gaming Commission) and Crocoslots (Curaçao Gaming Control Board) can legally accept Australian players, while being unable to advertise directly to Australia under local rules.
